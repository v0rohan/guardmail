How GuardMail works

A transparent look at what we check and how scoring works.

1. We read your inbox (read-only)

After you sign in with Google, GuardMail requests read-only Gmail access and pulls in your recent messages. We never send, delete, or modify anything in your mailbox.

2. Spoofing detection

We compare the sender's display name against a list of commonly impersonated brands (PayPal, Google, banks, and similar). If a brand name appears in the display name but doesn't match the sender's actual email domain, the message is flagged as a likely spoof.

3. Link sandboxing

URLs in the message body are extracted and checked against a list of keywords commonly used in phishing links (verify, login, secure, claim, portal, and similar). Matches are flagged as dangerous before you ever click them.

4. Risk scoring

Every message starts at a base score of 10. Points are added for an AI/keyword classification of scam (+45) or spam (+20), for detected spoofing (+25), and for each dangerous link found (+15 each), capped at 100.

5. AI reports & quizzes

For risky messages, GuardMail generates a plain-English threat report and a short multiple-choice quiz, so you learn to recognize the same warning signs on your own next time.
