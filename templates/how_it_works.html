How GuardMail works

A transparent look at what we check and how scoring works. Want to see it in action first? Try the demo — no account needed.

1. We read your inbox (read-only)

After you sign in with Google, GuardMail requests read-only Gmail access and pulls in your recent messages. We never send, delete, or modify anything in your mailbox.

2. Spoofing detection

We keep a list of commonly impersonated brands (PayPal, Google, Amazon, major banks, delivery services, and more) together with the domains they really send from. If a message claims one of those brands — in the sender name or buried in a lookalike domain — but wasn't sent from that brand's real domain, it's flagged as a likely spoof. Mail actually sent from the brand's own domain is never flagged.

3. Link checks

Every URL in a message is classified. Links to well-known domains pass as trusted — including their real sign-in pages. A link is marked dangerous when its address looks like a phishing setup: a known brand inside a domain that doesn't belong to it, phishing keywords in the registered domain itself, a raw IP address, a disguised destination, or lookalike characters. Shortened links get a caution label since they hide where they lead.

4. Risk scoring

Every message starts at a base score of 10. Points are added when the content is classified as a scam (+45) or spam (+20), when the sender looks spoofed (+25), and for each dangerous link (+15), capped at 100. Content classification uses AI when available, with a conservative keyword fallback that needs both a money hook and an urgency hook before it will call something a scam.

Nothing is a black box: open any message and expand "Why this score?" to see exactly which factors added up to its number.

5. AI reports & quizzes

For risky messages, GuardMail generates a plain-English threat report and a short multiple-choice quiz, so you learn to recognize the same warning signs on your own next time. Your quiz score is tracked in your browser — check it on the Settings page.

6. You have the final say

Heuristics aren't perfect. If GuardMail flags a sender you know is legitimate, mark it as safe — alerts for that sender are muted from then on. Trusted senders are stored only in your browser and can be managed from Settings. And when something really is a scam, report it to keep a record on your Reported Cases page.
