Privacy Policy

Effective July 6, 2026

What GuardMail accesses

When you sign in with Google, GuardMail requests read-only access to your Gmail inbox (gmail.readonly). We use it to fetch your recent messages and score them for phishing, spam, and spoofing risk. GuardMail cannot send, delete, or modify your email, and we never ask for broader access.

What we store

  • We do not store your emails. Messages are fetched and analyzed on demand while you use the dashboard, then discarded.
  • Your Google sign-in credentials live in a signed session cookie in your own browser, not in a database on our side. Signing out clears them.
  • If you report an email, we keep a record of that case — your email address, the sender, the subject line, and the risk score — so it can appear on your Reported Cases page.

Third-party services

  • Google: sign-in and Gmail access are handled through Google's OAuth and Gmail APIs, subject to Google's privacy policy.
  • Groq: to categorize risky messages and generate threat reports and quizzes, the sender, subject, and a truncated excerpt of the message body are sent to Groq's AI API for processing. Groq processes this content to return a result; see Groq's privacy policy.
  • Fonts and icons are loaded from Google Fonts and Cloudflare CDNs, which receive standard web request data (such as your IP address) when the page loads.

GuardMail does not run analytics or advertising trackers, and we never sell or share your data.

Revoking access

Signing out of GuardMail ends your session immediately. To fully revoke GuardMail's access to your Gmail account, remove it from your Google Account permissions — this takes effect right away.
